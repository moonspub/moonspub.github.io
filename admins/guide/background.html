Background checks

When moderating you may see a player that looks suspicious. Whether it's them making a joke that's a little too edgy or may be using dogwhistles.

Instead of immediately banning or taking action on them, it's worth doing a quick background check to see if they have history elsewhere. This can make your case against them stronger and reveal potential motives.

doing a quick check on steamhistory.net

The primary tool we use for quick background checks is a site called steamhistory.net. It will show the player's name history, avatar history, friends list, bans on a limited selection of other servers, and more. It may be incomplete however if the player has only recently been indexed.

We have a custom command in our server that can generate the URL for you. Simply run sm_steamhistory <username> in console. You can also open it in-game as an HTML MOTD using the sm_opensteamhistory/sm_openhistory commands.

digging a little deeper after the fact

If nothing shows up and you have the time, you can also use a little Google magic to find mentions of them on other sites. On the Steam history page there are various Steam ID types. Try running two searches: one with the SteamID64 in quotes and the normal Steam ID in quotes.

It also may be worth checking the Vault-F4 ban appeals/report forum. Sometimes this can surface detailed information about a player's ban there: https://www.vaultf4.com/forums/problem-player-reports-ban-contests.8/

Some other servers use Sourcebans and you can check them as well. For example, Vault-F4's Sourcebans lives at https://sb.vaultf4.com/

steamhistory.net does not check every Sourcebans instance out there, so be sure to check other ones.

Check the player's Discord and Steam for any links to other social media. Especially YouTube channels. There is a lot of insights from other social media sites that can inform your moderation decisions.
